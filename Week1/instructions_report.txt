Generated Work Instruction:
### Work Instructions: Perform Leak Test on Coolant System After Radiator Installation

#### Safety Precautions:
1. **Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)**: Always wear safety goggles, gloves, and a lab coat or coveralls to protect against coolant splashes and sharp edges.
2. **Work Area**: Ensure the work area is clean and free of any obstructions. Keep flammable materials away from the testing area.
3. **Hot Engine Caution**: Ensure the engine has cooled down before performing the leak test to avoid burns from hot components.
4. **Ventilation**: Ensure adequate ventilation in the work area to avoid inhaling any fumes from the coolant.

#### Required Tools:
1. Coolant pressure tester
2. Torque wrench
3. Safety goggles
4. Gloves
5. Recording sheet or digital device for data entry
6. Funnel (if adding coolant)
7. Clean cloth or paper towels

#### Work Instructions:

1. **Preparation**:
   - Ensure the vehicle is parked on a level surface and the engine is completely cool.
   - Gather all required tools and PPE.

2. **Visual Inspection**:
   - Inspect the newly installed radiator and surrounding components for any visible signs of damage or improper installation.
   - Check that all hoses and clamps are securely attached.

3. **Fill Coolant System**:
   - If necessary, use a funnel to fill the coolant system with the appropriate coolant mixture as specified in the vehicle’s service manual.
   - Ensure the coolant level is above the minimum mark in the reservoir.

4. **Connect Pressure Tester**:
   - Remove the radiator cap carefully to avoid any residual pressure.
   - Attach the coolant pressure tester to the radiator neck securely.

5. **Pressurize the System**:
   - Use the pressure tester to apply pressure to the coolant system. Set the tester to the specified pressure level (usually between 15-20 psi, but verify with the vehicle’s specifications).
   - Observe the pressure gauge for any immediate drop in pressure.

6. **Monitor for Leaks**:
   - Carefully inspect all connections, hoses, and the radiator for any signs of coolant leakage.
   - If leaks are detected, mark the area and report it to the supervisor for further action.

7. **Record Pressure Readings**:
   - After maintaining the specified pressure for 10-15 minutes, record the pressure reading on the recording sheet or digital device.
   - Note any changes in pressure during the test period.

8. **Acceptance Check**:
   - Compare the recorded pressure reading against the vehicle’s specifications.
   - If the pressure remains stable and within the specified range, the coolant system passes the leak test.
   - If the pressure drops below the specified range, the system fails the test and requires further inspection.

9. **Release Pressure**:
   - Carefully release the pressure from the coolant system using the pressure tester’s release valve.
   - Remove the pressure tester and replace the radiator cap securely.

10. **Clean Up**:
    - Wipe any spilled coolant with a clean cloth or paper towels.
    - Dispose of any waste materials according to company policy and environmental regulations.

11. **Documentation**:
    - Complete the recording sheet with the test results and submit it to the supervisor for review.
    - Ensure all tools are returned to their designated storage areas.

12. **Final Inspection**:
    - Conduct a final visual inspection of the coolant system to ensure everything is in order before closing the hood.

By following these steps, you will ensure a thorough and safe leak test of the coolant system after radiator installation.
